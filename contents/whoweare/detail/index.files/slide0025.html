Tobacco History in Japan (3)
New Times, New Tastes
The marked increase in the urban population of Japan during the 1920's was reflected in the steadily growing popularity of cigarettes. In 1930,
for the first time, more plain cigarettes were produced than "cigarette holder" type cigarettes. Japan's first domestically manufactured Europeanstyle
pipe tobacco appeared : the brand known as Momoyama.
In Time of War
During the World War II, countless shortages occurred, for goods and raw materials of all sorts were requisitioned for military use, and austeritie
programmes became increasingly strict. Wartime economies influenced processing and packaging; elaborate package designs gave way to plain
single-colour labels, while ordinary consumers were obliged to roll their own cigarettes from rationed allotments of shredded tobacco and paper.
In some cases, the severely limited supplies of tobacco leaves were even blended with leaves from other edible plants.
Post-War Rationing
Immediately after the end of the war, tobacco consumption was one of the few small luxuries that remained available to people of all economic levels.
Its unending popularity made it important as a source of government income, for the tobacco tax rate exceeded twenty per cent of the retail price.
Due, however, to wartime decimation of personnel and equipment, rates of production plummeted, and, like other consumer goods, tobacco products
were rationed, with the daily consumer quota set at three cigarettes per person.
The number of brands on sale in the late 1960's was approximately twice as large as the corresponding number available a decade earlier.
The king-size filter cigarette brand known as Hi-lite was unusually popular from the time when it first went on sale in 1960 ; it ushered in a new
fashion for filter cigarettes.
