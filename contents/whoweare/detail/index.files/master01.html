The New Amendment Bill of NHPA (KASH)
Background
Mr. Shin, Sang Jin (a member of the National Assembly, National Grand Party) introduced an amendment of NHPA on 9th Nov. 2007.
6 Major Areas: Promotion, Purchase, Place
A. Prohibition of all advertisements regarding tobacco and tobacco companies and prohibition of tobacco companies from directly and indirectly sponsoring all social, cultural, musical, and sport events 
B. Decrease of the number of tobacco advertisements from no more than 60 times a year to no more than 10 times a year 
C. Prohibition of all tobacco selling methods where buyers can have direct access to all tobacco products (including Display Ban)
D. Prohibition of making tobacco shaped products for minors
E. Prohibition of smoking areas and smoking within 5 meters from gates of public facilities
F. In using the fund for the projects under each subparagraph of Paragraph 1, the Minister of Health and Welfare may grant subsidies and make special supports for adolescent smoking prevention and treatment and rehabilitation programs.

Possible Next Steps.
This bill could refer to the Health & Welfare Committee of the National Assembly in December or February.
Packaging
Currently, Tar and nicotine levels must be indicated on one side of pack.
[Labeling carcinogen on packs]
•Background: The amendment bill of labeling carcinogen on pack was proposed by Assemblyman Seung-Jo Yang on January 30, 2007 and it was passed on November 22.
• Contents of the amendment bill : Carcinogen contents (which are arsenic, benzol, nickel, cadmium, naptalamine and polonium) must be indicated on the front and back sides of the packs with health warning.
•Specific wording, size, font, color of the text for carcinogen labeling will be announced by MOHW after passage of the bill.
- Size/ contents location: Inside of current health warning box (30%)
- Implementation grace period: 1 year
•Possible next steps
•Dec. 2008: As the Plenary Session passed the bill on Nov. 22, the bill will be promulgated by the President within 15 days of passage. As the grace period for implementation is 1 year, compliance with the bill will be required by Dec. 2008. 
•Requirement for manufactures: From the effective date (Dec., 2008), all products released from the bonded area must contain carcinogen labeling and advertisements also must contain carcinogen labeling.  However, it is not required that products released from the bonded area before the effective date contain carcinogen labeling
[Health Warning : Graphic health warning]
•Currently, health warning (Text) is required on cigarette packs and advertisements.
-Government provides specific wordings for health warnings and requires wordings to be rotated every 2 years.
-
•A member of the National Assembly introduced two amendment bills on 29th Jan. 2007 to make GHW mandatory on cigarette packs.
•Sub-Committee of Health & Welfare in National Assembly will consider both amendment bills submitted by Mr. Choi and MoHW and will probably consolidate the two bills for consideration by the Standing Committee. 
•However, it is uncertain when the Sub-Committee will submit the amendment bill to the Standing Committee due to the presidential election and national audit
•If the bills are approved at the Plenary Session, MOHW and MOFE will amend current Enforcement Regulations in order to prescribe size, wording and image of new health warning.
Place
Workplace with space greater than 1,000 square meters, transportation facilities, means of transportation with capacity to carry 16 people or more, restaurants with total space over 150 square meters must either be (1) designated as non-smoking or (2) smoking areas separated from non-smoking areas.
•Recently, some of the local government entities designated public places (such as parks, bus stops, beach, etc.) as non-smoking areas and more ordinances are on the way. 
•However, NHPA does not delegate authority to local governments to pass ordinances designating non-smoking areas.  Without such authority, it is not possible for the local government to fine people who smoke in designated non-smoking areas in violation of local ordinances.
•Meng, Hyung Gyu (a member of the National Assembly, Grand National Party) introduced an amendment bill on 17th May 2007 that provides authority for local governments to designate non-smoking area in public place and to fine people who are in violation of local ordinances.  The bill is still pending at the sub-committee of Health and Welfare since July.

Health Warning (Graphic health warning)
- Currently, health warning is required on cigarette packs and advertisements.

- MOHW has been promoting graphic health warning but there is no fixed target date.
Product
Under Article 2 of the Tobacco Business Act, the term "tobacco" means anything manufactured using leaves of the tobacco plant that is in a state suitable for smoking, chewing, or smelling. However, tar/nicotine level does not have to be indicated on packaging for smokeless products.
Purchase
Currently tobacco retailers are subject to distance restrictions (50 meter).
Promotion
Tobacco Business Act prohibits advertisement except by following means:
- in magazines (excluding those intended for women or juveniles)
- at retailer stores
- on flights
- sponsoring social, cultural, musical, sports events (no product advertisement other than the use of sponsor's name)
The strict application of TBA by government authorities increases the risk that communication or promotion will be deemed as illegal advertisement.
Ministry of Health & Welfare plans to prohibit tobacco companies from sponsoring sports events.
